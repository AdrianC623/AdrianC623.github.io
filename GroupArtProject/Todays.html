In the 2000s, rock music underwent a diverse and dynamic evolution. Post-grunge and alternative rock remained influential, with bands like Nickelback and Coldplay achieving commercial success. The decade saw a revival of garage rock with acts like The White Stripes and The Strokes, bringing a raw and minimalist sound to the forefront. Nu-metal, blending metal, hip-hop, and alternative elements, gained popularity with bands like Linkin Park and Limp Bizkit. Pop-punk and emo resonated with younger audiences, featuring bands like Green Day and Fall Out Boy. Indie rock continued to thrive, with artists such as The Killers and Franz Ferdinand contributing to the genre's eclectic landscape. The digital transformation and the rise of file-sharing platforms marked significant changes in how music was accessed and shared during this era. The 2000s also witnessed experimentation and boundary-pushing from bands like Radiohead, contributing to the ongoing evolution of rock music.
